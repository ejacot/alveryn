# Alveryn social launch

## Objective

The first campaign is not optimized for reach. Its target is:

- 10 completed registrations;
- 5 completed onboarding flows;
- 3 people who record a real workday;
- direct feedback from at least 3 users.

The Founder dashboard measures the funnel from Welcome visitor to the first workday.

## Positioning

**Primary message**

> Track the work. Know the hours. Understand the money.

**One-sentence description**

> Alveryn keeps hours, completed work, earnings and absences in one reliable record — even when every workday is different.

**Audience**

- people paid by the hour;
- people with multiple jobs or rates;
- people paid by completed units, deliveries, rooms, kilometres or tasks;
- people who currently reconstruct their month from notes, messages or spreadsheets.

Do not market Alveryn as payroll software, an employer workforce platform or an enterprise HR system yet. Those capabilities are not the current product.

## Video format

- 9:16, 1080 × 1920;
- 15–30 seconds;
- show the result in the first 2 seconds;
- one problem and one feature per video;
- large on-screen text, safe inside the center 80%;
- no private or real employment data;
- use the same clean demo account in every recording;
- end with `Try Alveryn free · alveryn.com`.

Post the same master video to TikTok, Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts without a platform watermark.

## First seven videos

### 1. More than an hourly timer

**Hook**

> Paid by the hour and per completed job?

**Screen recording**

Show one day containing a timed shift and a unit-based activity.

**Voice/text**

> A basic timer only records one part of the day. Alveryn keeps hours, completed work and the calculated earnings together.

**Caption**

> One workday. Different activities. One clear record. Try Alveryn free at alveryn.com.

### 2. The end-of-month problem

**Hook**

> Do you calculate your hours again at the end of every month?

**Screen recording**

Show the calendar and monthly totals.

**Voice/text**

> Record each day once. Your hours, workdays, earnings and absences stay ready when the month ends.

**Caption**

> Stop reconstructing the month from notes and messages.

### 3. Multiple jobs

**Hook**

> Two jobs. Different rates. One history.

**Screen recording**

Switch between two employments and their work types.

**Voice/text**

> Keep every job and rate separate without splitting your history across different apps.

**Caption**

> Built for work that changes.

### 4. Work paid per unit

**Hook**

> 24 deliveries. What did you actually earn?

**Screen recording**

Create a direct-per-unit entry and show the calculated result.

**Voice/text**

> Enter the completed quantity and let the configured rate calculate the result.

**Caption**

> Deliveries, rooms, pieces, kilometres or completed tasks.

### 5. Absences belong in the same month

**Hook**

> Workdays and vacation should not live in separate places.

**Screen recording**

Create an absence and show it in the calendar.

**Voice/text**

> Alveryn keeps work and configurable absences in the same monthly view.

**Caption**

> Your complete work month, not only the timer.

### 6. Founder story

**Hook**

> I built this because my work did not fit a simple timer.

**Screen recording**

Alternate between a short face-to-camera introduction and the product.

**Voice/text**

> Some days are paid by time, some by output, and some combine both. I am building Alveryn to make that work understandable.

**Caption**

> Building Alveryn in public. Honest feedback is welcome.

### 7. Fifteen-second product overview

**Hook**

> Your work, explained in 15 seconds.

**Screen recording**

Show onboarding, one record, the dashboard and calendar in quick cuts.

**Voice/text**

> Configure how you work. Record each day. Review hours, output, earnings and absences in one place.

**Caption**

> Try Alveryn free at alveryn.com.

## Publishing rhythm

Publish the first three videos on separate days. After that, publish three times per week. Respond personally to every relevant comment. Do not buy followers, views or engagement.

After each post, review:

- link visits;
- registration starts;
- accounts created;
- onboarding completions;
- first workdays created.

Continue the topics that create activated users, not merely the topics with the most views.
